# Behavioral Interview Rubric — STAR Method

## Overview
Behavioral questions assess past performance as a predictor of future success.
Responses should follow the **STAR** framework: **Situation → Task → Action → Result**.

---

## Scoring Criteria

### Excellent (85–100)
- Clearly identifies the **Situation** with specific context (team size, timeline, stakes).
- Defines the **Task** and personal responsibility unambiguously.
- Describes concrete **Actions** taken — uses first-person "I" language, details decision-making.
- Quantifies the **Result** with metrics (revenue, time saved, %, NPS, etc.).
- Demonstrates self-awareness — mentions lessons learned or improvements.
- Flows logically; no tangents; stays within 90–120 seconds.

### Average (50–70)
- Mentions a relevant situation but context is vague (e.g., "at my last company…").
- Task is implied but not explicitly stated.
- Actions are generic ("I worked with the team…") — lacks first-person ownership.
- Result mentioned but not quantified.
- Narrative is mostly coherent but includes minor tangents.

### Poor (0–40)
- No clear situation — begins with an abstract statement or opinion.
- Task and actions are conflated or missing.
- Uses "we" exclusively — unclear individual contribution.
- No result or outcome stated.
- Response is too short (<20 seconds) or unfocused (>180 seconds with no structure).

---

## Example Questions
- "Tell me about a time you resolved a conflict within your team."
- "Describe a situation where you had to meet a tight deadline."
- "Give me an example of a time you failed and what you learned."

## Key Evaluation Signals
| Signal | Weight | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| STAR structure adherence | 30% | All four elements present and sequenced |
| Specificity & metrics | 25% | Numbers, names, concrete details |
| First-person ownership | 20% | "I decided/built/led" vs. "we did" |
| Relevance to question | 15% | Directly answers what was asked |
| Conciseness & clarity | 10% | Within time bounds, no filler padding |
