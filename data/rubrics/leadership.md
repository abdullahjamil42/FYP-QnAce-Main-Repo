# Leadership Interview Rubric

## Overview
Leadership questions evaluate the ability to influence others, make difficult
decisions, and drive outcomes through people — not just personal execution.

---

## Scoring Criteria

### Excellent (85–100)
- Describes leading a team or initiative with clear scope (size, duration, stakes).
- Demonstrates decision-making under uncertainty — explains rationale.
- Shows influence beyond authority — persuasion, alignment, coaching.
- Mentions developing others (mentoring, delegation, feedback loops).
- Provides measurable team outcomes (shipped product, hit OKRs, team retention).
- Acknowledges what they would do differently — demonstrates growth mindset.

### Average (50–70)
- Describes a leadership scenario but focuses on personal tasks, not team dynamics.
- Decision-making is mentioned but rationale is unclear.
- Team outcomes are implied but not quantified.
- Little mention of developing others or handling disagreements.
- Mostly coherent but lacks the "why" behind actions.

### Poor (0–40)
- No evidence of leading people — describes individual contribution only.
- Confuses management (assigning tasks) with leadership (influencing direction).
- No team outcome discussed.
- Cannot articulate how they handled disagreement or pushback.
- Response sounds rehearsed or generic without personal substance.

---

## Example Questions
- "Tell me about a time you had to lead a team through a difficult change."
- "How do you handle a team member who is underperforming?"
- "Describe a decision you made that was unpopular. How did you handle it?"

## Key Evaluation Signals
| Signal | Weight | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Team-level impact | 30% | Led people/outcomes, not just tasks |
| Decision reasoning | 25% | Explains why, not just what |
| People development | 20% | Mentoring, delegation, feedback |
| Conflict navigation | 15% | Handled disagreement constructively |
| Self-awareness | 10% | Lessons learned, growth mindset |
